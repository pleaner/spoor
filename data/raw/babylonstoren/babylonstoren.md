## Positioning

One of the oldest Cape Dutch farms in the Cape Winelands, set at the foot of Simonsberg in the Franschhoek wine valley. Babylonstoren is a working farm, hotel, spa, restaurant group and botanical garden in one estate. Its defining proposition is a living farm-to-table philosophy — guests inhabit the farm itself, with access to the 5-hectare garden at all times, included guided tours, cellar and olive oil tastings, and daily farm activities. The Telegraph has called it a hotel "that changed your life." The estate targets couples, honeymooners, wellness travellers and families (except the Farmhouse cluster, which is adult-only). The Fynbos Cottages are specifically recommended for guests who want distance from day-visitor crowds.

**Farmhouse (/hotel/farmhouse)**
Positioned as adults-focused (not child-friendly). Combines "authentic architecture with modern design: refined and moody." Features a green communal library called the Butterfly Room housing a rare butterfly collection; loft above marketed as "one of the most beautiful honeymoon suites in Africa."

Accommodation units:
- **Farmhouse Suite** — 1 bedroom, 1 bathroom. King four-poster beds, moss-colored walls, marble en-suite bathrooms, walk-in cupboards, cast-iron stoves, MDF Italia sofas. Capacity: 2 guests.
- **Red Studio** — Studio with en-suite bathroom, kitchenette, fireplace, private entrance facing pétanque courtyard, outdoor shower in secluded garden. Capacity: 2 guests.

Promotion noted: "From June until end of July, stay three nights and only pay for two."

---

**Garden Cottages (/hotel/garden-cottage)**
"Relaxed yet well heeled." Transformed farm buildings adjacent to the 5-hectare cultivated garden. Cape Dutch architecture, contemporary interiors with Kartell, Magis, Philippe Starck, and Bouroullec Brothers furniture.

Units:
- **One-Bedroom Suite** — 2 guests. Open-plan, en-suite bathroom, lounge, fireplace, built-in bookshelves. (6 units)
- **One-Bedroom Cottage** — 2 guests. Kitchen, lounge, fireplace, bookshelves; contemporary glass cube kitchen extension. (3 units)
- **Two-Bedroom Cottage** — 3–4 guests. 2 en-suite bathrooms, kitchen, lounge, fireplace. Cape Dutch style. (4 units per website; rate card says 5 — see Collection notes)
- **Koornhuis** — 3–4 guests. 2 bedrooms with fireplaces, 2 en-suite bathrooms, kitchen, lounge. Adjacent to the 1777 Manor House. Not separately priced in rate card — see Collection notes.
- **Fowl House** — 2 guests. 1 bedroom, 1 bathroom, kitchen, dining area, fireplace. "For artistic souls and romantic retreats."

---

**Fynbos Cottages (/hotel/fynbos)**
Newly built Cape Dutch cottages positioned in the Western Cape's native fynbos habitat, adjacent to vineyards and orchards. Targeted at "adventurous travelers." Architectural Digest: "A little piece of paradise on earth."

Units:
- **One-Bedroom Cottage** — 2 guests. En-suite bathroom, fireplace, bookshelves, glass cube kitchen. (2 units)
- **Two-Bedroom Cottage** — 4 guests. 2 en-suite bathrooms, dining and kitchen in glass cube. (6 units)

Note from reviews: dgdgdg1 (Dec 2025) specifically recommended fynbos cottages "to avoid the crowds of day visitors."

---

**Manor House & Fynbos Family House (/hotel/manor-house)**
Both available as exclusive-use units only.

- **Manor House** — Historic Cape Dutch property dating to 1777. King-size beds in all rooms (two rooms offer twin option). Wood-burning stove in traditional farm kitchen. Library. Private lawn with games. Personalized stocked larder. Full garden access. (Capacity discrepancy: rate card says 3 bedrooms / up to 6 guests; website says 5 bedrooms / sleeps 10 — see Collection notes.)
- **Fynbos Family House** — 5 en-suite bedrooms, each with fireplace and marbled en-suite with freestanding bath. Fully equipped contemporary kitchen in glass cube. Private central courtyard with crystal pool "fed by a shallow channel." "An elegant, exclusive-use home" offering "a peaceful retreat like no other." Capacity: up to 10 guests.
