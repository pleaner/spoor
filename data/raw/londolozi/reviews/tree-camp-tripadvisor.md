## Worldbutterfly — Dec 2025 — 5/5

**Title:** (no title)

> "It's hard to overstate Londolozi's reputation and place in safari history. Though we knew enough to expect an excellent safari experience, we had no idea when we first arrived that we would be staying in such a special and iconic place. Our experience left us awed and honored--by the animals we were privileged to see, the people we met and the tiny piece of Londolozi's legacy we joined and have taken home with us. Lest you think I'm overdoing it, Londolozi and its founders, the Varty family, literally and figuratively wrote the book on melding wildlife conservation and hospitality, showing in both Southern and Eastern Africa, how preserving and re-wilding the landscape can pay for itself by attracting visitors from across the globe. This was a maverick idea back in the 1970s, akin to the early tech sages of Silicon Valley. And like those computer mavericks, the conservation mavericks of Londolozi saw something no one else did. Their legacy lives on in many leading safari lodges even if they are no longer tied to those properties financially. Londolozi remains their heart. As we were welcomed for our first visit, we joined people who were on their third, seventh, even 20th visit. Unless you are based in Southern Africa, everywhere in the world is very far away from Londolozi. Yet people return again and again (about 50% of all guests)! Nothing I'm about to say in this review is a greater testament than that. Despite all that, here is my review. Tree Camp is luxury and comfort surrounded by huge windows and a wrap-around deck. On one side of the windows is a full bedroom, full living room and big bathroom with a tub, double sinks and the best rain shower we have ever experienced. No lie. On the other side of the windows is the bushveld, full of birds, visiting monkeys and other animals, both visible and audible. Tree Camp is warmth and welcome, as personified by every single staff person we met, from our excellent butler Innocent to camp leads Hannah and Kate. The service was open and genuine, never cloying. Londolozi is not a property with anything to prove. Its standard of excellence imbues everything, a result of hard work, no doubt, but also a strong understanding on the part of everyone of what excellence at Londolozi means. Tree Camp, like all parts of the Londolozi whole, is centered on game drives, one in the early morning until about 9am (5am wake up call, coffee and a quick baked good: 5:30am, in the car), and one at 4:30pm until dark or a bit after. We were extremely privileged to have Patrick and Dorence as our guide and tracker dynamic duo. Because Londolozi is a private reserve, their well-maintained Land Rovers can go off the road--and off-road we went, following leopards and lions to better view them in their natural habitat. Patrick is a natural educator and knows when to drop facts and when to let the scene speak for itself. Dorence is such an experienced and well-trained tracker that it seemed like magic. We witnessed him find a cheetah by just the twitch of its tail from about 100m, an abandoned ostrich egg half-buried in the mud, and a chameleon almost completely covered by a leaf. Oh, and he and another guide found a full-grown male lion in the pouring rain and on foot. Amazing! We didn't opt for a private safari experience, but Londolozi also doesn't switch up guides/trackers mid-visit. So, most game drives we joined one or two other couples (fun!), and other times arrivals/departures worked out so we had Patrick and Dorence to ourselves. Our three full days (4 nights) felt like enough and also seemed like too little time. Maybe that's why everyone keeps coming back. In between game drives, there's lots of time to relax and enjoy the amazing food and cake-of-the-day. The daily yoga class was outstanding. I went every day I could. There is also a whole photography studio where you can rent fancy cameras (we didn't but many people did), a museum that explains the history of the place, and a very nice boutique. You can also take a tour of the whole 'backstage,' including the kitchen, Land Rover garage, and the village where all employees live while they are working at Londolozi. The village has a pre-school, weekly health clinic and a strong culture of community. We wondered if we were being fed some skilled marketing, but then ended up on a game drive with one of the doctors on rotation at the clinic who confirmed that everything lived up to the hype. We weren't really surprised. Londolozi Tree Camp is the definition of living up to the hype. I don't know yet if we'll be among the many who return (though we'd love to), but we are so happy to have been there. You will be, too."
