## avidskiier — Apr 2024 — 5/5

**Title:** "Don't Miss The Varty Experience!"

> "Our second trip to Varty Camp was , if anything , a bit better than our first (12 years ago) which was the trip of a lifetime , and our reason for returning at an advanced age . From the pickup at Skukuza airport by Vee , our tracker , to the welcome upon arrival at the camp , we were clearly in line for another unforgettable experience .and so it began ... with our first drive that afternoon with tracker Vee and ranger Jerry , who proved to be a stellar team ! The beauty of Londolozi game drives is that no two are the same . this time around we saw , among similar (but no less fascinating) things as first time , two things we hadn't seen first trip : a pack of ravenous wild dogs and a mother leopard with seldom seen triplets ! WE spotted the dogs walking down the road . Shortly afterward , a herd of Impala were spotted by both us and the dogs . The dogs took off in pursuit and Jerry drove the land as fast as he safely could down the road in the same direction , after a few minutes he cut into the bush to try to let us witness a kill . We kinda did but in the short time that had elapsed can impala had indeed been brought down ... but the pack was almost finished devouring it ! Incredible ! Apparently ,wild dogs have developed the ability to ingest and digest at incredible speed . This is thought to be an evolutionary development in response to larger predators stealing their kills .Can't imagine this is seen by many ! The other new experience came on the last afternoon drive . Jerry and Vee are aware of the female leopard with 3 cubs , having spotted the mother and one cub the previous afternoon and having heard the 3 had been seen . We spent a lot of time tracking the female , but no luck . Accordingly , Vee got out of the vehicle and tracked on foot . An hour later , he radioed that he and another tracker had found the lair in the deep bush and we drove into the bush and ... FOUND THEM ! We were able to spend 15 minutes watching and photographing them before the light faded : an INCREDIBLE , not to be forgotten , experience . Indeed , the whole Londolozi experience was , in itself , worth the long trip ! Everything in Varty is memorable and wonderful .There may be other Safari Camps offering great experiences , but i believe Londolozi has mastered the combination that offers a truly once in a lifetime experience , combining accommodation , food and drink great service and personal attention with the chance to witness life in the bush . Our undying thanks for this experience to the entire Varty Camp staff , with special mention of Jerry and Vee , camp managers Amber and Sam , butler Ryan whose service was over and above and executive Will , whose advice and attention were greatly valued ."
