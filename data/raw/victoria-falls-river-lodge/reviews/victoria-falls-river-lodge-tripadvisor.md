Final Verdict: We would totally return in a heartbeat. If you're considering a trip to Vic Falls, this is the place to stay for an experience that feels truly special.

### louise04 — August 2025 — 5/5
**Elegance on the Zambezi**

When we arrived at the airport in Victoria Falls, we were met by the hotel and taken to a boat ramp where we were loaded onto a boat, and we cruised to the hotel dock. There we were met in the lobby area with a refreshing drink and had an orientation about the hotel.

The hotel lobby is strikingly beautiful. It embraces the local motif along with an elegant and warm atmosphere. The view of the grounds opens up to the beauty of the river. Off the lobby is a hallway that leads down to a beautiful bar area, complete with an outdoor fire pit. The one evening, while enjoying the fire, a huge buffalo came over just below us. Made us realize how close and open the wildlife was all around us. We never felt threatened, however, and we were driven by golf cart to and from our tents. We were free to walk around during the day, but only escorted after dark, which we appreciated.

We enjoyed two of the river cruises during our stay where we watched elephants swimming in the river, hippos hiding beneath the surface except for their heads, crocodiles, and many, many bird species. The other days we took an exciting helicopter ride over Victoria Falls, visited an elephant rescue, and toured the falls on the ground. Wonderful experiences.

Every meal we had was fabulous. Everything is locally resourced and fresh. Great selections, too, on the varying menus.

We were in one of the newer "tents" our first nights, and I must say that they are anything but the tents I usually see. These were gorgeous, with elaborate bathrooms, including a shower and large soaking tub, both inside and outside the tent on the deck. Rooms were amazing with a large bed encased in a graceful mosquito netting canopy, nice sitting area, and refrigerator. Outside was a deck with a small plunge pool where we could watch the elephants go by. The water was too chilly for us, but in the summer months, it would be refreshing.

Our last night we moved to one of the older tents (numbered 1-4). It was very nice, too, but a step down from the first one. It did have two bedrooms, however.

I would highly recommend staying here for an immersive experience.
