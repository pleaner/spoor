## Review: Gypset | Jul 2025 | 4/5
**Title:** Marketing vs Reality
Returned to Cape Town after a safari, seeking a tranquil experience instead of the touristy waterfront area. Reviews and editorial praised the hotel's secluded location, boutique luxury design, and service. Upon arrival, discovered the hotel was nearly empty, and the experience felt creepy. The property was being used for events and photo shoots with skeleton staff. The 24-hour "butler" seemed more like a night attendant. Restaurant reservations were not savvy—not the bespoke service suggested by marketing. Everyone was nice and breakfast was delicious, but luxury pricing for a mid-range design hotel felt unjustified. The luxury terrace room was smaller than pictured with paper-thin walls. The bathroom felt uncomfortable with a toilet and shower separated by smoked glass. Furnishings showed wear, with lumpy pillows, stained sheets and towels apparently unchanged since the hotel's 2019 opening. Never seen this condition in a $600/night room, much less a $200 one. The hotel felt like "a tired Miami South Beach Hotel." The owner, who runs a large travel agency, should spend time upgrading the hotel rather than marketing it.
