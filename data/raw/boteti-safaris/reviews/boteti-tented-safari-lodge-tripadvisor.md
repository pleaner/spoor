### Paul V | Jun 2024 | 4/5
**Title:** Our stay was good but expensive
"We stayed for 5 days here. The accommodation was comfortable. The trips we did were all good and enjoyable. Our guide Val on the first day was amazing. The trip to Zola anga delta was also spectacular and well worth doing. And the helicopter ride was an incredible experience.

Slightly on the down side, one of the older guides had clearly had enough of tourists. On a freezing morning we had to persuade him to put the jeep covers down on a three hour early morning drive and he was quite grumpy- unlike any of the other staff who were all great.

You need warm clothing including at dinner because dinner is outside. The food is fine. You don't going a safari like this for a gourmet experience.

It is a shame that there was no water in the river. We were warned about this in advance in an email and that the so called 'complimentary' river trip could not take place. This was not described as complimentary in the advertising - indeed it was promoted as a part of the trip. So it isn't right to then say it is complimentary when it cannot go ahead. Of course it could not go ahead- but a replacement activity like a guided walk would have been a suitable alternative rather than just cancelling it. As the river is frequently dry, (we were told it dries up most years), it would be more honest to say this in the promotional material, especially as Boteti markets itself as a riverside experience.

We enjoyed a fabulous experience and it was worthwhile. Botswana - and this safari - is definitely expensive, even taking into account that a helicopter ride was included. South Africa is a lot cheaper but didn't offer the variety of this safari. You pays your money and takes your choice."

### RaRaRachelC | Feb 2024 | 4/5
**Title:** Very good, just needs some adjustments
"We were a bit worried booking Boteti as the place has very mixed reviews. We booked with Nedine at Boteti and communications werre fast and efficient. We were picked up as arranged from our previous camp by David who was our driver/guide for the duration of our stay. The camp is near a community although the camp itself is gated. This means you may see goats, donkeys and the occasional cow wander past. It didn't bother us one bit. There is also a track to the camp which is very sandy and I imagine self drivers may struggle as it is about 10-15 mins drive in bumpy, rough conditions.

Despite reading some reviews about bugs, our tent was immaculate, not a bug or ant in sight which suggests staff have dealt with the bug issue or are treating rooms. The 'rooms' were well thought out with space for luggage and mosquito nets were also over beds (although they were a bit small and therefore the net was very close to our head and feet and with the fan it would blow on our face). It would be better to replace these with the curtain type and although staff did a turn down at night we still struggled with these nets and they became a pain with one of us getting up in the night it would disturb the other one because of the way the net hung.

One thing was it was soo hot. I know its probably difficult to get aircon in a tent but the fan was like a hair dryer blowing hot air. Maybe keeping the curtains and window blinds shut would help but staff routinely opened these in the morning so by time we got back from Moremi or our sunset cruise it was well over 40 degrees in the tent. Due to furniture it sometimes made it difficult to access the tent blinds.

Showers were hot and powerful and very clean. It would be interesting to know if there is something they use in the water and if it is treated with something or not. Its hard to describe but we felt some kind of residue on our skin. I've read another review which mentioned a funny water smell which we didn't notice but there was something different about the water I'm just not sure what.

Food was good and staff were friendly however the restaurant needed a fan or AC as it was uncomfortable. We didn't use the pool but others did. It didn't look that clean (debris etc in it and the colour was a bit odd).

The included sunset cruise was a bit of a disappointment. Its not a mokoro cruise rather just taken to part of the Boteti River by the camp who have their own plastic canoe type boat where a local will then take you out (Boteti supply some juice and water in a coolbox). It's not the most attractive area (plastic bottles discarded on side of riverbank) and the actual time on the water was being taken 10 mins down the river, get out and drink our drinks where there is rubbish whilst the local smoked something questionable on the bank whilst we were stood watching the sunset (which actually wasn't sunset as we were too early) and then less than 10 mins cruise back to where we started for the short drive back- which meant we actually missed sunset. On the plus we did see hippos and crocs but with less than 20 mins on the water and no sunset were were disappointed (it is not its advertised 1 hour or 2 hours). We did get to see sunset the following evening and there is a nice seating area by the river.

We did the full day (over 12 hours) game drive to Moremi with David. It's a long day on some very, very sandy and bumpy sand roads however was excellent. David found a pack of 6 cheetahs which was the absolute highlight of our trip. He is excellent as a guide and his knowledge and expertise is a reason to book Boteti."

### Whereintheworlf | Nov 2021 | 3/5
**Title:** A pleasant stop-over just outside Maun, but beware the web site!
"This small camp is right on the river and offers tented accommodation. There is a very attractive pool deck, bar and dining area. Two thoughtful touches are the small deck at the riverside, where you can enjoy the last rays of the day. and the simple but good boma dinner, which offers traditional food. The food was good., thanks to a smiling and hard-working lady chef.

The web site is impressive, but unfortunately, some of the beguiling words under the heading 'The Experience' are a straight deviation from the truth. The camp is definitely not in a 'remote location' 'away from all man made distractions'.. It is actually on a land concession a few kilometres from Maun. To add to this, the approach to the camp hardly constitutes 'pristine and untouched natural surroundings'. We looked hard for such surroundings as we wound our way past impoverished human habitations, rickety fences and domestic animals. The cows and donkeys roaming around the camp, and the riverside area, cow bells a-jingling, certainly added a quirky touch to this prisitne environment.

Apart from the hyperbole on the web site, you can do excursions, like a mokoro ride on the river or on the Delta. We went to Moremi for the day - it is quite far on a rough road - and the staff got up very early to arrange packed food and to get the very nice vehicle ready. Well done to guide Relaxed for his practicality and organisation skills.

There is room for improvement regarding the tents. They are extremely hot in the afternoons. Too hot to be in. One appliance in the room didn't work. It was replaced but the replacement didn't work. So, more attention to maintenance is needed."
