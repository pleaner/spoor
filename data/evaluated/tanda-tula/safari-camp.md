## Value

Tanda Tula Safari Camp is a single fully-inclusive product, so its benchmark ADR
reflects party shape and the Stay-Longer-for-Less special, not a room ladder. A
**Couple** in a Safari Suite prices at **ZAR 66,630 / USD 3,578 per night** at rack,
dropping to **ZAR 54,030 / USD 2,901** in the Nov–Dec stay-3-pay-2 window (~19%
seasonal swing). For that the guest gets "all meals, laundry, soft drinks, house wines,
local beers & spirits… game drives & guided walks; transfers to & from Rockfig
Airstrip" (raw dossier, *Inclusions*) across a redesigned 9-suite camp on "55,626
hectares of traversing". The pricing reward sits in the **Family Suite**: a flat base
rate of R94,500 for 1–3 guests means three adults price *below* three Safari-Suite
guests (the search picks the Family Suite — see the Group/Family configs), and the
child rate (R15,750, 6–16) is barely a sixth of the suite base. A **Group** of 8
adults runs **ZAR 216,120–266,520 / night** across four Safari Suites. The
age-banded **sustainability levy** (R1,815 pppn for 12+, R910 for 6–11) is itemised on
top of every ADR and never discounted.

**Assumptions the pricing script had to make (each a real gap):**

- Single flat 2026 season; every night priced at the 2026 rate.
- Safari Suite max 3 (2 adults + 1 child 6–16); no third-adult rate.
- Family Suite max 4: base covers 1–3 guests, then one additional guest.
- A lone occupant must be 17+.
- Stay-pay windows are the Wetu-confirmed offers; the website's extra 2026 9 Jan–30 Apr window is not applied.
- Honeymoon (proof of wedding) and Aerial Safari (a value-add) never lower the rate.
- Per-vehicle entrance fee excluded (fly-in assumption).

The rate card is unusually clean on rates and policy, but two checklist marks need
correcting and the assumptions expose real gaps:

- **Check-in / check-out — present, but not in the rate card.** The checklist marked
  it absent because it scans the rate-card section; the times (check-in 12:30,
  check-out 11:00) are in *Wetu Fast Facts* (raw dossier), so this is fine.
- **Cancellation terms — genuinely absent.** Neither the rate card nor the dossier
  records a cancellation / no-show policy; this is a true gap for anyone needing to book
  (a usable rate card should carry it).
- **A stay-pay window discrepancy** is the most material pricing gap. The Wetu offers
  give Nov–Dec and Jan–Mar windows, but *Collection notes* record that the website also
  advertises a **2026 9 Jan–30 Apr** window "not present as a separate Wetu offer". The
  script applies only the Wetu-confirmed windows, so Jan–Apr 2026 ADRs above may be
  **overstated** if that website offer is real — flagged, not assumed.
- **Suite occupancy and the single-age rule** were not stated numerically and had to be
  assumed (Safari Suite max 3; Family Suite max 4; lone occupant 17+). The 2027 figures
  exist as **rack only** (no STO27 sheet), so trade ADRs cannot be computed for 2027.

## Fit

Tanda Tula is built for **families and multi-generational groups** more than any other
persona: it is one of the few cards here with a **dedicated Family Suite** (two en-suite
bedrooms, base rate 1–3 guests) and a **minimum child age of 6** with a real child rate
(R15,750, 6–16). The benchmark Family persona (2 adults + children 6/10/14) is feasible
in all 12 months at **ZAR 133,265–164,765 / night** — though note the *cheapest* valid
config the engine finds splits an adult into a single Safari Suite alongside a
one-adult Family Suite; a real family would more likely book one Family Suite plus a
Safari Suite at slightly higher cost, so treat the family ADR as a keen lower bound.
**Couples** are well served (Safari Suite, honeymoon special) and **groups** up to 8
fit comfortably across the 7 Safari + 2 Family Suites. The **2-night minimum** and
fly-in orientation (complimentary Rockfig transfers; FedAir-only airstrip) make it a
considered, not a casual, booking.

## Self-competitiveness

- **Rack-vs-trade spread:** STO25 is 25% off the accommodation, but with the levy
  undiscounted the **ADR spread is ~23.6%** (Couple, open season: ZAR 66,630 rack vs
  50,880 STO). Consistent across personas.
- **Seasonal spread:** ~**19%** between the Nov–Dec stay-3-pay-2 ADR (54,030) and the
  open-season rack ADR (66,630). As with the rate card, there is no separate high/green
  rate — the swing is entirely the stay-pay special.
- **Single-supplement burden:** a solo guest's ADR is **ZAR 49,065**, a **~47% uplift**
  over a sharing couple's per-person cost (33,315/night), driven by the flat 50% single
  supplement (R47,250 vs R31,500 pppn). Solo travel carries a clear premium.
