## Value

Makanyi prices on a single per-person-per-night sharing rate, so the benchmark ADR
moves with party size and the Stay-4-Pay-3 special rather than with a room "tier" — all
feasible personas are placed in **Luxury Suites** (R21,000 pppn), the entry rate,
because the Pool and Marula suites both cost more per head (R23,000). A **Couple** sits
at **ZAR 43,240 / USD 2,322 per night** at rack in the open season, falling to **ZAR
34,840 / USD 1,871** in Jan–Mar when the Stay-4-Pay-3 free night applies (a ~19%
seasonal swing — see *Self-competitiveness*). For that the guest gets a fully inclusive
stay — "all meals and beverages including alcoholic, South African house wines and
MCC's", "two daily game drives", gym, laundry and seasonal bush walks / star gazing
(raw dossier, *Rate card → Included*) — on a reserve running "only three safari
vehicles across 8,000 hectares". The value is therefore uncrowded, high-touch game
viewing, not a graded room ladder: stepping up to a Pool Suite (private plunge pool) or
the 366 m² two-bedroom Marula Suite buys space and privacy, not extra inclusions. The
mandatory **R620 pppn** conservation levy is itemised on top of every ADR above and is
never discounted, even under the special.

**Assumptions the pricing script had to make (each a real gap):**

- Single flat season for the whole 2025/26 validity; dates outside it reuse it.
- Conservation levy R620 pppn is the 2025 figure, assumed to hold.
- No guests under 12 (exclusive use not assumed) → such parties are infeasible.
- Honeymoon 50%-off needs proof of marriage (soft) and is never auto-applied.
- Per-vehicle gate levy excluded (fly-in assumption).

The rate card is structurally complete on the priced essentials, but two checklist
ticks need correction and the surfaced assumptions point at real holes:

- **Minimum stay — actually absent.** The checklist auto-ticked on the special's
  "minimum of 3 nights" language, but Makanyi imposes **no general minimum stay**; the
  3-night floor belongs only to Stay-4-Pay-3 (raw dossier, *Special Offers*). The
  pricing script accordingly enforces no minimum.
- **Validity / rate currency is the biggest gap.** Rates and the levy are quoted only
  for **01 Apr 2025 – 31 Mar 2026** (and the levy as a **2025 calendar-year** figure;
  raw dossier, *Not Included*). The script must reuse that flat rate for any 2026+
  benchmark date and assume the R620 levy holds — both flagged. *Collection notes*
  confirm "Rates for 01 Apr 2026 onward are not available" and "2026 levy rates are not
  confirmed", so the 2026 ADRs above carry that caveat.
- **No child rate under 12.** The card states "No children in any suite under 12 years
  of age unless the lodge is booked on an exclusive basis", so the **Family persona is
  infeasible in all 12 months** (children 6 and 10) — not a pricing gap but a genuine
  product limit.
- **Single flat season** means the script cannot exercise per-night season lookup here;
  any future seasonal card would.

## Fit

Makanyi fits **couples and honeymooners** first: eight suites, a Fast-Facts **minimum
child age of 12**, an explicit Honeymoon special, and positioning toward "honeymooners,
couples, wildlife photographers" (raw dossier, *Positioning*). The benchmark bears this
out — the Couple persona prices cleanly at ZAR 43,240/night, while the **Family persona
is infeasible** (no under-12s) unless the whole lodge is taken on exclusive use. The
one **Marula Suite** (two en-suite bedrooms, sleeps 4) is the sole family-capable unit
and is pitched at "families*, groups of friends or two couples", the asterisk again
being the under-12 rule. A **Group** of 8 adults is well served — eight suites, priced
at ZAR 139,360–172,960/night across four Luxury Suites — and group/exclusive-use terms
exist in the card. Photographers are explicitly courted (private vehicle, professional
tutor). It is **not** a fit for young families on a normal booking.

## Self-competitiveness

- **Rack-vs-trade spread:** the STO rate is 25% off the accommodation, but because the
  R620 levy is never discounted the **ADR spread compresses to ~24.3%** (Couple, open
  season: ZAR 43,240 rack vs 32,740 STO). The trade margin is healthy and uniform
  across personas.
- **Seasonal spread:** modest and special-driven, not tiered — **~19%** between the
  Jan–Mar Stay-4-Pay-3 ADR (34,840) and the rest-of-year rack ADR (43,240). There is
  no high/green-season rate split in the card itself.
- **Single-supplement burden:** heavy. A solo guest's ADR is **ZAR 32,120**, a **~49%
  uplift** over the per-person cost of a sharing couple (21,620/night), reflecting the
  flat 50% single supplement on the Luxury Suite — and 100% on the Marula. Solo
  travellers pay a real premium here.
