## Value
Private Granite Suites is the **most expensive and most exclusive** of the five Londolozi
camps on a per-person basis: one suite type at **R54,000 pps** (versus R48,450 at Tree
and Pioneer, R34,200–39,400 at Founders). A couple's ADR is **ZAR 109,149 (≈ USD 5,861)**
January–November, rising to **ZAR 118,715 (≈ USD 6,375)** for a 15-December arrival. A
solo traveller pays **ZAR 68,074.50** ADR. There is no family or group tier at all — both
the **Family and Group personas are infeasible in every month** (see Fit), so the camp's
benchmark is the couple rate alone.

That rate buys the same fully-inclusive Londolozi product (all meals, all drinks bar
premium, two daily drives, walks, transfers, laundry, WiFi, VAT) in a markedly more
private, design-forward setting: three standalone suites for "the exclusive use of six
Guests", each with "a private, heated swimming pool" and "an outside riverview bath", a
"silver, charcoal and elephant grey" palette, "a sunset river bar and sunken cocktail
lounge…suspended high above the Sand River", and Relais & Châteaux status (dossier Website
& Wetu). The Guest Conservation Contribution (R957.50 pppn, capped at 3 nights — the
higher "Tree, Granite and Pioneer" band) is the only line added on top of the per-person
rate.

**Assumptions the pricing script had to make (each a real gap):**

- Two rate cards apply; each night priced under its own card (2026 through 15 Dec 2026, 2027 thereafter), so a 15-Dec stay straddles the boundary.
- No trade/STO rate published on the Londolozi cards; STO shown equal to RACK.
- Guest Conservation Contribution capped at 3 nights and applied to all guests (the card neither age-bands nor exempts children).
- Private Granite Suites take no guests under 16 unless the camp is booked exclusively; exclusive-use buyout is not assumed, so under-16 parties are infeasible (children 6–16 would pay the adult rate under buyout only).
- Private Granite Suite max occupancy 2 (couple suite; no third-person rate on the card); three suites give a camp capacity of six guests, so parties that cannot be seated within 3 suites × 2 are infeasible.
- Park entrance fees, road transfers, the noise-impact levy and private vehicles are excluded under the benchmark's fly-in assumption.

The two ✗ marks repeat the group pattern: **inclusions / exclusions** are documented via
the dossier's pointer to the group-wide list, and **check-in / check-out** sit in Wetu
Fast Facts (13:30 / 11:00) — both outside the scanned rate-card section. The genuine
absences are **minimum stay** (none on the card) and **cancellation terms** (deferred to
the uncaptured Group Policy).

The material assumptions are few here because the product is simple. **No trade/STO rate
is published**, so RACK equals STO. The two consequential modelling choices both concern
exclusivity. First, **the camp is treated as not accepting under-16s**: the card says
"Children between the ages of 6 and 16 years can only be accommodated if the entire camp
is booked", and since a buyout is not the standard benchmark booking, the Family persona
is ruled infeasible (under a buyout those children would pay the adult rate). Second,
**each suite is modelled at max 2 with a 6-guest camp cap** ("exclusive use of six
Guests", three suites, no third-person rate), which is what makes the 8-adult Group
infeasible. Both are faithful to the dossier rather than inferred conveniences. The
conservation levy is applied to all ages.

## Fit
Granite is the **adults-only, special-occasion couples camp** at the top of the
Londolozi price ladder. The dossier is explicit on the target guest — "the discerning
traveller who appreciates the finer details", "ideal for honeymooning couples or small
groups celebrating special occasions" — and on the restriction: "This private camp does
not accommodate children under the age of 16." That single rule makes the **Family
persona infeasible all 12 months**, and the six-guest exclusive-use cap makes the
**8-adult Group infeasible** too; only couples (and, by extension, up to three couples
taking the whole camp) are priceable.

It suits, narrowly and well: honeymooners, anniversary couples, and a small group of up
to six adults wanting to buy out an intimate, design-led river camp. It is the wrong camp
for any family with children under 16 (use Founders or Varty) and for larger adult groups
of seven or eight (use Founders, Varty, or Tree's four-suite buyout). The trade-off a
guest is buying is privacy and exclusivity over flexibility — three suites, six people,
no shared-camp bustle.

## Self-competitiveness
**Rack-vs-trade spread: none (0%).** No STO/trade rate is published; RACK equals STO.

**Seasonal spread: flat with one December step.** The single year-round card holds Couple
ADR at ZAR 109,149 January–November, with December at ZAR 118,715 (**+8.8%**) — the
15-December straddle into the 2027 card (R54,000 → R59,950 pps). The step is slightly
smaller in percentage terms than the other camps' ~10% because Granite's 2027 increase
(11.0% on the rack rate) is the group's lowest, and only four of the five benchmark nights
fall on the new card.

**Single-supplement burden: ~25%, as stated** ("25% single supplement from date of
arrival"). A solo guest's ADR is ZAR 68,074.50 versus ZAR 54,574.50 per head sharing
(half of 109,149) — a **24.7% premium**, the small dilution below 25% being the flat
conservation levy. With one suite type and no children, there is no internal tier or
family rate to trade across; the only lever is sharing versus single occupancy.

## Reputation

### Reputation summary

| Source | Rating | Reviews | Sample / span |
|---|---|---|---|
| TripAdvisor | 5 / 5 | 106 total | 13 quoted (partial, top-sorted) |

TripAdvisor shows a stated overall of **5.0 / 5 across 106 total reviews**. The **13
reviews quoted are a partial, top-sorted sample** (pages one and two), so they sit at the
favourable end of the distribution. Within that sample **12 of the 13 are 5/5 and one is
4/5** — the single non-perfect entry being uptown_girlNY (Jan 2012).

Recency is moderate: the quoted reviews run from a 2009 entry to **ramin S (Jul 2024)**,
the newest, with a cluster around 2020–2022; there is no 2025–26 review in the captured
set. Praise is emphatic on the suites and service — Nakkiran (May 2021) wrote "the PGS
camp at Londolozi is probably the best game lodge in South Africa … The room/suite is
massive", and TxBamaEx (Dec 2022) noted "The Granite Camp suites are second to none and
Kelly makes no detail how minor is overlooked." The **only criticism in the captured
sample** is the lone 4/5 from uptown_girlNY (Jan 2012), who praised the "impeccable
service" but found "wine selections were limited and food quality was merely acceptable,
preventing a perfect rating." That is a single fourteen-year-old data point and no similar
complaint recurs elsewhere in the sample; beyond it, **no recurring negative theme is
present** — which, given the top-sorted pull, means "nothing surfaced here", not proof of
none across all 106.
