## Value
Varty is the **most affordable** of the five camps and, with Founders, one of the two
genuinely family-capable ones. Its entry product is the **Standard Chalet at R27,950
pps** — the lowest per-person rate in the group — with two **Superior Chalets** a tier up
at R34,200. A couple's ADR is **ZAR 56,589 (≈ USD 3,039)** January–November, rising to
**ZAR 62,298 (≈ USD 3,345)** for a 15-December arrival; the search seats them in a
Standard Chalet. A solo traveller pays **ZAR 35,282** ADR.

All three personas are feasible. A family of two adults + three children prices at **ZAR
113,522–124,954 (≈ USD 6,096–6,710)** ADR (the 6- and 10-year-olds at the R13,975 child
rate, the 14-year-old at the adult rate, across two Standard Chalets), and a group of
eight at **ZAR 226,355–249,190 (≈ USD 12,155–13,382)** ADR across four Standard Chalets —
the cheapest benchmark Group figure of any Londolozi camp. The rate is fully inclusive on
the standard group terms (all meals, all drinks bar premium, two daily drives, walks,
transfers, laundry, WiFi, VAT), with the Guest Conservation Contribution (R573.99 pppn,
capped at 3 nights — the lower "Founders and Varty" band) the only separate line. Each
chalet has "its own private swimming pool, outdoor shower and outdoor dining sala", and
guests have access to the group's gym, Healing House, Cubs Den and the historic 1926
Sparta Camp within Varty.

**Assumptions the pricing script had to make (each a real gap):**

- Two rate cards apply; each night priced under its own card (2026 through 15 Dec 2026, 2027 thereafter), so a 15-Dec stay straddles the boundary.
- No trade/STO rate published on the Londolozi cards; STO shown equal to RACK.
- Guest Conservation Contribution capped at 3 nights and applied to all guests (the card neither age-bands nor exempts children).
- Varty Camp has two room types (8 Standard Chalets, 2 Superior Chalets); the cheapest valid seating across all ten chalets is chosen.
- Children 6+ welcome (minimum guest age 6). Child policy encoded verbatim: two children 6–11 sharing each pay 50% of the adult rate; one child 6–11 alone in an inter-leading chalet pays the full adult rate (no single supplement); a child 12+ pays adult rates.
- No explicit per-chalet capacity is published; modelled as max 2 adults plus up to 2 children (6–11) per chalet (couple/family oriented, no third-adult rate).
- Park entrance fees, road transfers, the noise-impact levy and private vehicles are excluded under the benchmark's fly-in assumption.

The two ✗ marks are the familiar artefacts: **inclusions / exclusions** are documented
through the dossier's pointer to the group-wide list (it even notes Varty-specific items
— "One group yoga class per day held at Founders Camp" and "Fitness Room at Varty Camp"),
and **check-in / check-out** are in Wetu Fast Facts (13:30 / 11:00). The real absences are
**minimum stay** and **cancellation terms** (the uncaptured Group Policy).

The notable gap specific to Varty is **per-chalet capacity**, which the dossier does not
state: Wetu's Room Types give "no per-chalet guest capacity or bed configuration." The
script therefore assumes max 2 adults + up to 2 children (6–11) per chalet, drawing on
the camp's couple/family positioning and the inter-leading child rules ("Two children
(6 – 11 years) sharing pay 50% of the adult rate each when sharing an inter-leading
chalet"). That assumption underpins the Family and Group seatings, so a materially
different real capacity would shift those ADRs (the Couple and Single figures, which use
one chalet, are unaffected). As across the group, **no trade/STO rate is published** (RACK
= STO) and the conservation levy is applied to all ages. The campfire-age discrepancy in
the dossier (website "over 90 years" vs Wetu "over 86 years") is positioning copy and does
not affect pricing.

## Fit
Varty is the **founding, family-and-heritage camp** — "The heart and soul of Londolozi",
the original camp where "the campfire has burned … for over 90 years", with the 1926
Sparta Camp inside it. Crucially it is family-capable: children are welcome from age 6
("the emphasis on family heritage means that children always have a place at Varty Camp"),
so the Family persona is feasible all 12 months (113,522 ADR), as is the 8-adult Group
(226,355 ADR) across the eight Standard Chalets. With ten chalets it has the most capacity
of any single Londolozi camp.

It is the best all-rounder of the five and the natural choice on price: it suits families
with children, larger adult groups, and value-conscious couples who want the full
Londolozi experience at the lowest entry rate, with a "pleasingly down-to-earth quality."
The two Superior Chalets give a modest step-up for a couple wanting a little more. It is a
weaker fit only for guests specifically seeking adults-only seclusion or the most
design-forward suites — Tree, Granite and Pioneer target that end. One reviewer's note
(below) that they "Preferred Tree Camp's non-adjoining rooms over Varty's connected units"
is a fair flag for couples sensitive to inter-leading layouts.

## Self-competitiveness
**Rack-vs-trade spread: none (0%).** No STO/trade rate is published; RACK equals STO.

**Seasonal spread: flat with one December step.** A single year-round card rate holds
Couple ADR at ZAR 56,589 January–November, with December at ZAR 62,298 (**+10.1%**); the
Family and Group rows step up by the same ~10.1%. As elsewhere this is the 15-December
straddle into the 2027 card (Standard Chalet R27,950 → R31,500 pps), not a seasonal
surcharge.

**Single-supplement burden: ~25%, as stated** ("25% single supplement from date of
arrival"). A solo guest's ADR is ZAR 35,282 versus ZAR 28,294.50 per head sharing (half
of 56,589) — a **24.7% premium**, diluted slightly below 25% by the flat conservation
levy. The **internal room-tier spread** is the camp's other lever: the Superior Chalet
(R34,200) runs ~22% above the Standard (R27,950), a wider in-camp step than Founders'
~15%, giving Varty the broadest own-camp price range of the group.

## Reputation

### Reputation summary

| Source | Rating | Reviews | Sample / span |
|---|---|---|---|
| TripAdvisor | 4.9 / 5 | 260 total | 21 quoted (partial, top-sorted) |

TripAdvisor shows a stated overall of **4.9 / 5 across 260 total reviews** — the largest
review base of the five camps. The **21 reviews quoted are a partial, top-sorted sample**
(pages one and two), so they describe the top of the distribution; within it, **20 of the
21 are 5/5 and one is 4/5** (Stephanie p, Jun 2024).

Recency is excellent: the quoted reviews run back to Nov 2020 but cluster heavily in
2024–26, the newest being **John H (Jan 2026)** and **Deborah C (Dec 2025)**. The praise
is dense and concrete on people and the deck setting — John H (Jan 2026): "The heart and
soul of Londolozi are the people"; Deborah C (Dec 2025): "I sobbed the day we left and I
have never left a place with such a yearning to return"; avidskiier (Apr 2024) judged that
"Londolozi has mastered the combination that offers a truly once in a lifetime
experience." Dietary care recurs positively across several reviews (Heidi E, Nov 2023, on
"gourmet level gf options"; Ron R, Jul 2024, on vegan options).

The **only criticism in the captured sample** is the lone 4/5 from Stephanie p (Jun 2024),
a vegetarian guest who "experienced menu inconsistencies initially but noted improvement"
and "Preferred Tree Camp's non-adjoining rooms over Varty's connected units" — a fair,
specific flag about the inter-leading chalet layout rather than a service complaint, and
she still rated the guiding "off the charts exceptional." No other negative theme recurs;
with a top-sorted sample of 21 of 260, this should be read as "little surfaced here", not
as evidence of none across the full base.
